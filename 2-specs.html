Developer Test

The job posting IS the interview

Your job post is designed to filter out bad candidates. It should contain 3 parts:

  • In your job post, explicitly tell the developer to respond with a certain phrase in the subject. You don't want developers who don't read.

  • A survey of tech questions about development practice -- These are mostly to test communication skills
  • A simple test for the developer. It should be:
    • Simple
    • Doable in less than 2 hours
    • Clearly defined, but with one or two ambiguous points

Developer Test (Part 2)

"Give the candidate just enough rope to hang himself"

Test for:
  • Developer Competency
  • Speed
  • Responsiveness
  • Communication
  • Process
  • Comprehension
Expect:
  • 10 in 50 developers to respond
  • 3 in 10 respondents to succeed
  • 1 in 3 to be available
Yep, it sucks!

Use A Trusted Advisor

Get a nerd to watch your back. Who?

  • Friends, if you have them
  • Other tech CTOs, alcohol buys friends

    Picking up the tab for a night of boozing buys goodwill and will save you money from getting screwed. Consider this an insurance policy

How to use them

  • People are busy. Make it easy for them. Do all the prep work, you just need them to sign off.
  • Specific asks:
    1. Help design a developer test
    2. Be the final filter on hiring
    3. Broad tech architecture (if it matters)
    4. Choose reasonable milestones
    5. Evaluate code after milestone delivery

I Want The Best of the Best

Get in line

The market is highly inefficient, so there ARE great deals on awesome devs at depressed prices. However, it takes lots of time, effort, and skill to find them.

Advice: Get "good enough" -- waiting for "the best" might mean never building.

Collaborating With Developers

  • Require version control (git or something)
  • Use a ticketing system (codebase HQ, trello)
  • Daily reports (whenever they work, they should send you a summary)
  • Do not skimp on small resources. If they need a host, get it for them. A domain? Buy it. You're spending 1000s of dollars, don't let 10 dollar expenses trip you up.

Examples from the spam Mike got. This is one of my favorite examples because he got so many messages and they:

  1. come from different free email addresses - Mike has received over 50 variants of this email message. Not ONE of the sending email addresses is repeated. Each comes from a random address from a free service like hotmail. The spammers probably have a bot that creates these free accounts in bulk then they send these via smtp.
  2. use nonsense names for the senders - This I don't get. It strikes me as just lazy. "Pleet Boonstra" isn't even a believable name (in the US). Why not something more plain, like "Kate Smith"?
  3. have a large number of different domains that all lead to the same place. Notice that each of the domains at the bottom of the emails are all different. In fact the actual domain it will click through to is never even the same as the anchor text. But they all end up serving the same files from the same server.
  4. use automated text rewriting, i think, to evade spam detection (probably something like thebestspinner). What this means is that a human writes the initial email, then there's a program that finds possible synonyms for a lot of the words/phrases, then randomly generates hundreds, or thousands of different variants of the same text. This is a commonly used tactic in SEO as well as email marketing.

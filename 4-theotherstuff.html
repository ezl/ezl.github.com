Product Development is the Easy Part

"If you build it, they will come" -- Some Liar


Many entrepreneurs initially believe that getting product built is their largest obstacle to success.

Unfortunately, for most, getting product built is the easy part. The hard part is convincing people to use/buy it.

How Search (Sort Of) Works

  • Web sites and the links to one another form a "graph" of nodes (sites) and directional connections (links) between them. Visualize it as giant spider web.
  • Google grabs a handful of marbles and throws them over the spider web.
  • The marbles follow the links from each page, leaving a few at every stop.

    • Page Rank is a measure of where the marbles collect.

    SEO is the practice of controlling that.

    Of course, it's fancier than this and the content matters as well.

Blogs

  • Write a blog that interests your target market
  • Find blogs that your target market reads
  • Ask other bloggers to guest post on your blog
  • Ask to guest post on other blogs
  • Ask bloggers to review/promote your product

Forums

  • Find industry forums and message boards (reddit counts)
  • If there's a community, engage it -- comment, contribute, don't only go there to self-promote -- people can see right through it
  • If people can't tell the difference between self promotion and a trusted forum participant that loves you product, there is no difference.

Social

  • Twitter + Facebook - Have real conversations with people interested in your space. This is a ton of work, but its a methodical climb to the top
  • Linked In - if you can use it, it's awesome. People self identify by education and profession.

Traditional PR
